male vs female findings





* Across all 1,006 male Kata performances, male athletes performed 29 unique kata. Across all 964 female Kata performances, female athletes chose the exact same number of kata to perform: 29. 
* Of those, 23 kata were performed by both genders. 6 kata were performed exclusively by males, and 6 exclusively by females. See Figure G-2 for the full breakdown and Figure G-3 for the exclusive kata.
* The female field showed much greater concentration at the top. A single kata, Papuren, dominated with 198 performances, dwarfing the next most performed, Suparinpei, by 69 (129). No comparable outlier exists on the male side, where the three most performed kata cluster closely together (108 / 104 / 86). See Figure G-1.
* Male kata performances, on average, scored .178 higher than female Kata performances. The average score given to any male kata performance was 8.132. For female kata performances, it was 7.954.
* Male kata performance scores have 7 low outliers and 7 high outliers; female scores have 4 low outliers and 4 high outliers. The female dataset contains more extreme lows, while male scores cluster more tightly at the bottom but reach higher peaks at the top.
* The single highest score recorded in female competition was 9.22 (Chatanyara Kusanku), nearly matching the male peak of 9.28 (Chibana No Kushanku).
* Female kata scores were more tightly clustered within each kata. The typical female kata had a score standard deviation of about 0.23, compared to 0.33 for the typical male kata (Figure FK-6) — meaning that for any given female kata, athletes' scores landed closer together. 
* Female athletes almost entirely avoided Intermediate kata. Just 1.6% of female performances (15 of 962) were Intermediate kata, spread across only 5 different kata. Male athletes leaned on Intermediate kata noticeably more — 9.4% of their performances (89 of 947), across 9 different kata. At the elite level, the female field concentrated more heavily on Advanced kata than the male field did.
* Male kata competition was dominated by Kakeru Nishiyama (Japan), with a win rate of 100.0% and nine gold medals out of nine tournaments. Female kata saw an extremely competitive dynamic at the top, with Grace Lau (Hong Kong) and Maho Ono (Japan) as the two clear frontrunners. Lau averaged a score of 8.43 (win rate: 91.7%; 9 medals, 5 golds). Ono averaged a score of 8.44 (win rate: 88.5%; 9 medals, 3 golds).
