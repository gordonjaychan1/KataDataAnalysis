female kata findings



* The mean score for all 964 Female Kata this season was 7.954, and the median score was 7.94, indicating an approximately symmetrical distribution.
* The most performed Female Kata was Papuren with 198 performances. The second most performed was Suparinpei with 129. See Figure FK-1 for a full breakdown.
* 8/964, or 0.830%, of Female Kata performances were outliers. There are 4 low outliers: 6.14, 6.20, 6.38, 6.98 and 4 high outliers: 8.88, 8.88, 8.96, 9.22.
* Performances vs. Average Score (Figure FK-4):

  * As Kata have more performances, their Average Score regresses to the global mean (7.954).
  * In Figure K-4, there is a large contrast between the Average Score of Kata with greater than 60 performances and the Average Score of those with less than 60 performances. All Kata with at least 60 performances have Average Scores relatively close to 7.954 (within .116 points), but Kata with less than 60 performances have Average Scores that deviate heavily (by as much as .282 points).
* Score Differential (Figure FK-5):

  * An interesting pattern emerges when comparing Score Differential (how much athletes score on a Kata relative to their personal average) to Win Rate. Tomari Bassai had the second lowest differential (-0.205), meaning athletes performing it scored well below their personal average, so you would assume it has a low win rate. However, its win rate is the second highest of any kata: 92.3%.
  * Furthermore, you might assume that the kata with the highest differential, which is Sochin (+0.210), would have a high win rate, yet its win rate was 0% across its three performances.
  * This apparent contradiction reveals a key limitation of the data: the Score Differential measures how an athlete scores on a Kata relative to their own average, not relative to their opponent's score. Tomari Bassai's negative Differential likely reflects that it is chosen predominantly by elite athletes whose personal averages are already very high; even scoring "below average" for them is competitive. Upon checking Tomari Bassai's detail card, you will find that 9/13 of its performances are by Grace Lau, the Female athlete with the most Gold medals. Sochin's high differential but low win rate, meanwhile, suggests that Aya Hesham, the athlete who performed it three times, scored well in isolation, but faced opponents who scored even higher.
* Standard Deviation vs. Number of Performers (Figure FK-6):

  * Kata performed by more athletes tend to show higher score variance, since a wider ability range is represented.
  * Kata with only 1–2 performers have no meaningful standard deviation.
  * **Kururunfa** has a notably **LOW** Standard Deviation for a Kata with 22 performers.
* Average Score by Tournament (Figure FK-7):

  * Judging was remarkably consistent.
  * The Average Score by Tournament only varied by up to .178 points, which is quite low across 964 performances.
* Kata Tiers

  * A staggering 98.4% of Female Kata performances were Advanced Kata; just 1.6% were Intermediate Kata.
  * Female athletes chose 5 Intermediate and 24 Advanced Kata to perform.
  * Notably, Female athletes only chose 5/56 of the Intermediate Kata to perform, indicating that only a small percentage of Intermediate Kata are viable at this highest level of Kata competition.
* Win rate should be interpreted with caution: it reflects the outcomes of specific matchups and is influenced by opponent strength and bracket luck, not kata choice alone. See Figure K-3 for win rates across all kata.
* Small sample sizes for rarely performed kata make their statistics unreliable. Kata with fewer than 5 performances may show extreme win rates or differentials simply due to limited data, and should not be over-interpreted.
